--- title: "Spacetime Grid" section: "gravity" ---

For the purposes of simplicity, spacetime is often represented as a 2D grid that bends into the 3rd dimension. In the absence of any massive objects, spacetime appears as a flat, square grid. However, massive objects bend spacetime and cause it to sag, like a bowling ball on a trampoline. This effect is commonly called gravity. When viewed from a top-down perspective, this effect seems to curve and stretch the straight lines in the grid.
