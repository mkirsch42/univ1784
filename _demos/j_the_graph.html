--- title: "The Graph" section: "graphing" ---

The vertical axis of the graph (ct) represents the progression of time in a stationary reference frame. The horizontal axis (x) represents a snapshot of one-dimensional space at a given time; in other words, all events on a single line parallel to the x-axis are perceived as simulataneous. Events are represented by a point, while paths are represented by lines. A vertical line represents a stationary object, since it remains at the same x-value in space throughout time. Units are chosen on the graph such that the path of an object moving at the speed of light is at a 45-degree angle.
