--- title: "Time Dilation" section: "graphing" ---

Since the scales on a moving reference frame's axes are stretched, the graph can portray time dilation. According to the stationary reference frame, events T and T' occurs simulataneously, so ct' appears to experience time slower. However, since the moving reference frame's spatial axis is angled, it sees T' occuring before T. In fact, when T occurs for them, it appears as though their clock is faster. As shown before, both reference frames argue that the other one experiences time slower!
